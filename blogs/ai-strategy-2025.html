How Early-Stage Startups Should Think About AI in 2025

A framework for founders navigating the build vs. buy vs. partner decision

With AI tools proliferating at an unprecedented pace, early-stage founders face a genuinely new strategic question: should you build AI capabilities in-house, buy a third-party solution, or partner with a specialist?

The answer, as with most things in strategy, is "it depends" — but the variables that matter most are often misunderstood.

The Core Trade-off

Building AI capabilities in-house gives you control, customisation, and a potential competitive moat — but it demands significant engineering talent and time. Buying accelerates go-to-market but creates dependency on a vendor's roadmap and pricing. Partnering offers a middle path, but requires aligned incentives and clear governance.

For most early-stage startups, the right answer in 2025 is to buy or partner first, then build selectively as you learn. Here's why.

Why Speed Beats Perfection Early On

The biggest risk for an early-stage company isn't building the wrong AI architecture — it's building the right AI architecture for a product nobody wants. You need to get in front of real users, generate real feedback, and iterate quickly. Off-the-shelf AI tools let you do that without the overhead of a model team.

Once you've validated that users genuinely value the AI-powered element of your product — and you understand exactly which capabilities differentiate you — then it makes sense to invest in proprietary development.

When to Build Proprietary AI

There are clear signals that suggest you should begin building in-house AI capability:

Your AI is your product. If the intelligence itself is the primary value driver — not just a feature — then vendor dependency becomes an existential risk. You need control over the model, the data, and the outputs.

You have proprietary data. Fine-tuning or training on data your competitors don't have access to is one of the few remaining sources of durable AI advantage. If you're accumulating that data, use it.

Vendor costs are unsustainable at scale. Token costs add up fast. If your unit economics don't work at scale with current API pricing, you need to build your way to efficiency.

Practical Advice for Founders

Start with a clear-eyed audit of what AI actually enables in your product. Is it the core experience, or is it an accelerant for something else? That answer should drive your build-vs-buy decision more than any technology trend.

Then move fast. The worst outcome is spending six months evaluating options while your market moves. Make a defensible decision, instrument it well, and plan to revisit in 90 days.
